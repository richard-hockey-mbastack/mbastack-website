TOOLS

Tools to deliver CXM

CXM is embedded throughout our process:

"Dream accelerator" workshops

We start off with new clients or on big new briefs with a session designed to get moving full steam ahead with minimal delay. This involves sharing key thoughts from desk research, investigating the brand, business objectives, and audiences. We frequently involve external experts and actual customers (!) to provide inspiration.

"CXM maps"

We've developed a customer journey model building on work by Google and McKinsey in particular. This helps us to think through the problem to inform the challenges our experiences need to overcome and to organise the roles of media and content / messaging accordingly.

"Visible data" and "visible tech"

are clear and present boxes to be completed in creative briefs. This is to ensure these specific angles are considered and embedded in our work. There will be no need for post-rationalisation as to how the creative work has been informed.

We'll develop "CXM narratives"

to support propositions – showing how the brand can appeal to its audience from acquisition through consideration, into in-life scenarios. This doesn't replace the proposition per se – specific touchpoints still need to do specific things, but the CXM narrative helps us understand how this forms part of a coherent whole.

The "Three chairs" concept

has been shamelessly borrowed from Amazon's "empty chair for the customer" approach. We use the chairs of data, tech and creativity as stimulus to thinking about a problem, prompting and probing for solutions to the challenge.
